Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud**

UPI/online payment fraud is a cybercrime in which fraudsters deceive users into transferring money through digital payment platforms such as Google Pay, Phone Pe, or Paytm. These scams often rely on social engineering techniques to manipulate victims.

A common scenario begins when the victim receives a call or message from someone posing as a buyer, bank official, or customer care representative. The fraudster gains the victim’s trust and sends a “collect request” or a QR code, claiming it is needed to receive money. The victim, unaware of the process, enters their UPI PIN, which actually authorizes a payment instead of receiving funds. In other cases, victims are tricked into clicking phishing links that mimic official banking apps, where they unknowingly enter sensitive details.

College students, elderly individuals, and small business owners are frequent targets because they may lack awareness or frequently use digital transactions. Fraudsters also target people posting ads on online marketplaces, pretending to be interested buyers.

The consequences can be severe. Victims may lose significant amounts of money instantly, with limited chances of recovery due to the speed of transactions. Additionally, such incidents cause emotional distress, loss of trust in digital systems, and potential misuse of personal data for further fraud.

This case highlights the importance of digital literacy, awareness, and cautious behavior while using online payment systems to prevent financial and personal loss.
